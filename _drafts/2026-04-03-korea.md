[South Korea][1] (officially The Republic of Korea) is a [highly developed][4] nation of 51.6 million located in [East Asia][6]{:target="_blank"}.

The whole Korean peninsula covers an area of 223,516 km², which, for comparison, is smaller than New Zealand at 263,310 km². South Korea occupies about 100k km². The Seoul metro area is home to over 25 million, making it one of the most densely populated places on Earth.


## Economy

South Korea's economy [ranks 12th-largest in the world][7] with a GDP of 1.88 trillion USD. Per capita, that's 36k USD or 61k at PPP [according to World Bank numbers from 2024][5]. The IMF shows Korea as 15th in 2026, having been overtaken by Spain, Mexico and Australia.

Following occupation and war, South Korea grew from one of the world’s poorest countries in the 1950s to one of the richest today. That period of rapid export-led economic growth is called the [Miracle on the Han River][10]. South Korea is now a global leader in consumer electronics, display panels, and semiconductors, with 60% market share in memory chips. Other key industries are auto manufacturing, shipbuilding, petrochemicals, steel, and batteries.


## Health, Education, and Culture

South Korea's fertility rate is the lowest in the world - 0.72 children per woman and falling as of 2023, which is well below the replacement rate of 2.1. It has one of the world's highest suicide rates and highest per-capita alcohol consumption. On the other hand, life expectancy at 83.5 is among the world's highest and Koreans have one of the world's best healthcare systems.

South Korea investments in education and is one of the top-performing OECD countries in literacy, mathematics, science and engineering. Seoul National University and KAIST are well placed in the global rankings.

South Korea is among the most ethnically homogeneous societies in the world, but also has one of the fastest-growing foreign-born populations. Korean culture and cuisine are popular around the world.


## Korean Language

[Korean is the native language][3] for about 81 million people. In the south, the language is known as Hangugeo (한국어). The Hangul (한글) alphabet uses 14 consonants and 21 vowels - 10 basic vowels which combine to form 11 more complex vowels.


### Hangul

![Hangul](/images/korea/korean-alphabet.gif)
_Graphic from the web site: [Amazing Talker][9]_

Letters are arranged in syllable blocks consisting of an initial consonant, a vowel, and an optional final consonant called batchim (받침). Special rules apply to the last position or batchim. Some letters change pronounciation. Also, 11 consonant digraphs, ㄳ, ㄵ, ㄶ, ㄺ, ㄻ, ㄼ, ㄽ, ㄾ, ㄿ, ㅀ, ㅄ, and the doubled consonants ㄲ and ㅆ can appear in the final position.

![Hangul syllable blocks](/images/korea/syllables.jpg)
_The word for the Korean language is used to illustrate the structure of syllable blocks in a [paper from Kyuhong Shim et al][8]._

For example, you may want to write the phrase "Oppan Gangnam Style", which would be: 오빤 강남스타일 where the last bit, seu-ta-il, is as close as you can get to style. [Go deep on _Gangnam Style_ with someone called The Language Nerd][201].

### Beginner Vocabulary

- 안녕하세요 (an-nyeong-ha-se-yo) - hello
- 안녕히 가세요 (an-nyeong-hi-ga-se-yo) - good bye
- 감사합니다 (gam-sa-ham-ni-da) - thank you

- 네 (ne) or 그래요 (gue-lae-yo) - yes
- 아니요 (a-ni-yo) - no
- 아마도 (a-ma-do) - maybe

- 좋아요 (joh-a-yo) - that's good
- 맞아요 (maj-a-yo) - that's right
